History

1854

Louis Vuitton founded his eponymous brand in Paris, initially focusing on creating high-quality luggage and travel goods. He introduced innovative designs, such as flat-topped trunks that were easier to stack.

1860s

Vuitton patented the canvas material used for his trunks, which was lightweight and waterproof. This led to the iconic Monogram canvas being created in 1896, featuring the interlocking "L" and "V" alongside other symbols.

Late 19th Century

The brand gained recognition among aristocrats and wealthy travelers, becoming a symbol of luxury and sophistication. The first Louis Vuitton store opened in London in 1885.

1920s - 1930s

The brand expanded its product line to include handbags, accessories, and leather goods. The Speedy bag was introduced in 1930, becoming an iconic staple.

1950 - 1960s

After World War II, Louis Vuitton experienced a renaissance, bolstered by increased demand for luxury goods. New styles and collaborations began to emerge.

1987

Louis Vuitton merged with Moët Hennessy, forming LVMH, the world’s leading luxury goods conglomerate. Bernard Arnault became a pivotal figure in its expansion.

2000 - present

Louis Vuitton has continued to innovate and collaborate with contemporary artists and designers, enhancing its status as a luxury icon. It remains a leader in fashion, known for its craftsmanship, exclusivity, and timeless appeal.

Our Founder

Louis Vuitton

Full Name: Louis Vuitton Malletier
Date of Birth: August 4, 1821
Place of Birth: Anchay, France
Early Life:

  • Came from a modest background.
  • Moved to Paris at age 16 to apprentice as a box-maker and packer.

Founding of Brand:
  • Year Founded: 1854
  • First Store: Opened in Paris.

Notable Achievements:
  • Gained popularity among the European aristocracy.
  • Appointed as the official trunk-maker for Empress Eugénie, wife of Napoleon III.
  • Introduced the iconic monogram canvas in 1896 featuring the LV logo.

Death: February 27, 1892

Legacy:
  • The brand continued under his son, Georges Vuitton.
  • Louis Vuitton is now one of the most prestigious luxury brands in the world, known for leather goods, fashion, accessories, and fragrances.
  • Recognized for innovation and collaborations with various artists and designers.
